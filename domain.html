Academic Review

Research Domain

An in-depth analysis of the educational landscape, existing gaps, and our proposed methodologies.

1. Literature Survey

Recent advancements in immersive technologies have demonstrated significant improvements in student engagement and knowledge retention. Virtual reality enables learners to interact with abstract concepts in a spatial environment, reducing cognitive load and enhancing comprehension. Gamification further strengthens motivation by incorporating rewards, challenges, and feedback loops into the learning process.

However, existing solutions are largely based on Western curricula and lack contextual relevance for Sri Lankan students. Additionally, many systems rely on passive content delivery rather than interactive problem-solving experiences.

2. Research Gap

Critical omissions identified in the current educational technology ecosystem.

Despite global advancements, there is a clear absence of curriculum-aligned, culturally contextualized virtual reality platforms tailored for Sri Lankan middle school education. Existing platforms lack bilingual support, offline accessibility, and alignment with national syllabus requirements.

3. Research Problem

"Students in Sri Lanka face difficulties in understanding abstract STEM concepts due to limited access to practical learning environments and an over-reliance on traditional teaching methods."

4. Research Objectives

Platform Development

To design and develop a gamified VR learning platform aligned with the national curriculum.

Immersive Engagement

To improve student engagement through immersive and interactive experiences.

Knowledge Retention

To enhance conceptual understanding and long-term retention.

Cultural Relevance

To provide a culturally relevant educational environment.

5. Methodology

A structured technical and academic approach to solving the problem.

The project follows a Design-Based Research approach, incorporating iterative development and continuous evaluation. The system was developed using Unity 3D and deployed on Meta Quest 3 hardware. Interactive modules were designed based on curriculum mapping and integrated with gamification elements such as tasks, rewards, and feedback mechanisms. A centralized telemetry system was implemented to track user interactions and performance metrics.

7. Core Components

Science

Interactive Science Learning in VR

Explore ecosystems, energy systems, and plant biology through immersive virtual experiments. Students learn by doing-observing cause and effect, conducting simulations, and understanding complex scientific concepts in a safe, engaging environment.

Mathematics

Visual & Gamified Math Experience

Transform abstract mathematical concepts into interactive 3D experiences. From problem-solving challenges to spatial visualization, students grasp formulas and logic through engaging, hands-on VR activities.

Engineering

Learn How Things Work

Understand real-world engineering principles by building and experimenting with virtual systems. Students explore mechanics, structures, and problem-solving through interactive simulations and design-based challenges.

Technology

Future Skills Through Immersive Tech

Introduce students to modern technology concepts through interactive VR environments. From digital systems to innovation thinking, this component builds foundational knowledge for the tech-driven world.
